The state of personal AI agents

Agents are harder than chat

Meta’s Mark Zuckerberg publicly acknowledged that AI agent development has not accelerated as expected, despite massive infrastructure investment — highlighting how difficult it is to move from capable models to reliable agents that actually do work on computers.

Interface vs. operation

OpenAI leadership continues to frame ChatGPT as the interface layer for future agents, but the market is learning that operating real software — logins, forms, multi‑step workflows — is where systems diverge.

Why computer use matters

Google’s Gemini Spark launch underscores that browser‑ and desktop‑level control is becoming table stakes for serious assistants — not just conversation quality.

The cost problem

Repeated computer‑use workflows are expensive if every run starts from scratch. Without reuse, agents stay impressive demos instead of daily tools.

How today’s assistants compare

ChatGPT

A world‑class general assistant with strong reasoning and ecosystem momentum. Moving toward agents, but still primarily conversation‑first.

Gemini

Google is aggressively pushing agentic features and browser‑native computer use, as seen with Gemini Spark on macOS.

Grok

An opinionated assistant with real‑time and social context from X. More about immediacy than durable workflows.

Siri

Deeply embedded in Apple devices and voice‑first. Recent improvements show promise, but complex multi‑app workflows remain constrained.

Folk

Primarily a CRM‑centric tool. Relevant as context in the automation market, but not positioned as a general personal agent.

Orchids

An experimental entrant in the agent space, illustrating the breadth of approaches but without major recent public milestones.
